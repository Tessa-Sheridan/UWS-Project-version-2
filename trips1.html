Trip Ideas

Need some inspiration? With so much to see it can be hard to figure out where to go. Here's some suggestions for trips in Scotland

Longer Itinerary Ideas

❮ ❯

The North Coast 500

The North Coast 500 is a 516 mile scenic route through the north of Scotland which makes for a perfect road trip. Starting in Inverness, the route takes you along the beautiful west coast, up to some of the most northerly points in Scotland, and back towards Inverness on the east coast. It showcases the best that Scotland has to offer, with dramatic mountains, remote sandy beaches, castles and wildlife. At least 5-7 days is recommended to give you time to explore and relax!

❮ ❯

The West Highland Way

The perfect Scottish holiday for avid hikers, the West Highland Way is a 154km (96 mile) long-distance walking route. Beginning in the town of Milngavie, close to Glasgow, it winds its way past the beautiful shores of Loch lomond, the unspoilt wilderness of Rannoch Moor and the stunning mountains of Glencoe. A minimum of 5-7 days is recommended, depending on your fitness levels, and you'll find plenty of accomodation and campsites along the way.

❮ ❯

Island Hopping

Experience the best of the Scottish islands on an island hopping trip. There are hundreds of islands to choose from, and each has something unique to offer. You can create itineraries to suit everyone - from a few days to a couple of weeks. Why not try the Outer Hebrides for stunning white sand beaches and scenery? Or the Shetland islands for a truly remote escape with abundant wildlife and even a chance to see the northern lights in the winter
